Category I Factions & Societies The "Who"
For world-builders: These are the beings that form tribes, guilds, courts, and settlements. They have leaders, motivations, and internal politics. Every one of these can be talked to, traded with, manipulated, or fought as part of an organized force. Start here when designing the social layer of your world.

Power Hierarchy — weakest to strongest
Kobold→ Goblin→ Hobgoblin→ Gnoll→ Bugbear→ Ogre→ Lizard Man / Troglodyte ✦ regional variant
A classic dungeon stacks these by level: Kobolds on floor 1, terrorized by Goblins on floor 2, who answer to Hobgoblins, who pay tribute to a Bugbear chief. Each descent is harder and has a reason to be.
Category II The Wilderness & Beasts The "Outside"
For world-builders: These creatures are driven by instinct, hunger, and territory — not ideology. They form the backbone of overland random encounter tables and define what the wilderness feels like to travel through. Use these to make the journey dangerous, not just the destination.

Category III Dungeon Terrors & Magical Weirdness The "Deep Dark"
For world-builders: The bizarre, the magical, and the unnatural. When stocking a dungeon you reach here for a specific puzzle, a trap with legs, or a terrifying boss. Many of these creatures exist nowhere else — they are the dungeon. Use these sparingly; each one should feel like an event.

Category IV The Undead The "Restless"
For world-builders: The undead interact with unique game mechanics — specifically the Cleric's Turn Undead ability — and instantly establish a gothic, death-haunted tone for tombs, crypts, and cursed ruins. A party with no Cleric should fear these encounters. Note that Wight causes permanent level drain — this is one of the most dangerous abilities in the game regardless of HD.
